# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (Directly Relevant to Story CoT)

1. **Narrative-of-Thought: Improving Temporal Reasoning via Recounted Narratives** (narrative_of_thought_temporal_reasoning.pdf)
   - Authors: Xinliang Frederick Zhang, Nick Beauchamp, Lu Wang
   - Year: 2024 (EMNLP Findings)
   - arXiv: 2410.05558
   - Why relevant: **Most directly relevant paper.** Proposes using narrative-form reasoning (Narrative-of-Thought/NOT) as an alternative to standard CoT for temporal reasoning. Shows narrative CoT outperforms standard CoT on temporal tasks.

## Foundational CoT Papers

2. **Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models** (wei2022_chain_of_thought_prompting.pdf)
   - Authors: Jason Wei, Xuezhi Wang, Dale Schuurmans, et al.
   - Year: 2022 (NeurIPS)
   - arXiv: 2201.11903
   - Why relevant: Foundational CoT paper. Establishes the baseline that Story CoT aims to improve upon.

3. **Self-Consistency Improves Chain of Thought Reasoning in Language Models** (wang2022_self_consistency_cot.pdf)
   - Authors: Xuezhi Wang, Jason Wei, Dale Schuurmans, et al.
   - Year: 2022 (ICLR 2023)
   - arXiv: 2203.11171
   - Why relevant: Key CoT improvement method (majority voting over multiple paths).

4. **Automatic Chain of Thought Prompting in Large Language Models** (zhang2022_automatic_cot.pdf)
   - Authors: Zhuosheng Zhang, Aston Zhang, Mu Li, Alex Smola
   - Year: 2022
   - arXiv: 2210.03493
   - Why relevant: Automated CoT generation, relevant to automating story-form demonstrations.

## CoT Variants and Alternatives

5. **Tree of Thoughts: Deliberate Problem Solving with Large Language Models** (yao2023_tree_of_thoughts.pdf)
   - Authors: Shunyu Yao, Dian Yu, Jeffrey Zhao, et al.
   - Year: 2023 (NeurIPS)
   - arXiv: 2305.10601
   - Why relevant: Structural alternative to linear CoT; contrast with narrative-form reasoning.

6. **Beyond Chain-of-Thought: Graph-of-Thought Reasoning** (yao2023_graph_of_thought.pdf)
   - Authors: Yao Yao, Zuchao Li, Hai Zhao
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2305.16582
   - Why relevant: Another structural CoT alternative.

7. **Buffer of Thoughts: Thought-Augmented Reasoning** (yang2024_buffer_of_thoughts.pdf)
   - Authors: Ling Yang, Zhaochen Yu, Tianjun Zhang, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2406.04271
   - Why relevant: Template-based reasoning; narrative schemas could serve as thought templates.

8. **Chain-of-Thought Reasoning Without Prompting** (wang2024_cot_without_prompting.pdf)
   - Authors: Xuezhi Wang, Denny Zhou
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2402.10200
   - Why relevant: Shows CoT can emerge without explicit prompting; relevant to understanding CoT mechanisms.

9. **Contrastive Chain-of-Thought Prompting** (chia2023_contrastive_cot.pdf)
   - Authors: Yew Ken Chia, Guizhen Chen, Luu Anh Tuan, et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2311.09277
   - Why relevant: Uses positive+negative exemplars; applicable to story-form CoT.

10. **Chain of Code: Reasoning with LM-Augmented Code Emulator** (li2023_chain_of_code.pdf)
    - Authors: Chengshu Li, Jacky Liang, Andy Zeng, et al.
    - Year: 2023
    - arXiv: 2312.04474
    - Why relevant: Code as reasoning medium; comparison with narrative as reasoning medium.

11. **Implicit Chain of Thought Reasoning via Knowledge Distillation** (deng2023_implicit_cot_distillation.pdf)
    - Authors: Yuntian Deng, Kiran Prasad, Roland Fernandez, et al.
    - Year: 2023
    - arXiv: 2311.01460
    - Why relevant: Distilling CoT into internal representations; could apply to narrative reasoning.

12. **Diffusion of Thoughts** (ye2024_diffusion_of_thoughts.pdf)
    - Authors: Jiacheng Ye, Shansan Gong, Liheng Chen, et al.
    - Year: 2024
    - arXiv: 2402.07754
    - Why relevant: Alternative generation paradigm for CoT reasoning.

## CoT Analysis and Understanding

13. **Chain-of-Thought Reasoning in the Wild Is Not Always Faithful** (arcuschin2025_cot_not_faithful.pdf)
    - Authors: Iván Arcuschin, Jett Janiak, Robert Krzyzanowski, et al.
    - Year: 2025
    - arXiv: 2503.08679
    - Why relevant: Questions CoT faithfulness; Story CoT may be more/less faithful.

14. **Invalid Logic, Equivalent Gains** (schaeffer2023_invalid_logic_reasoning.pdf)
    - Authors: Rylan Schaeffer, Kateryna Pistunova, Samar Khanna
    - Year: 2023
    - arXiv: 2307.10573
    - Why relevant: Shows invalid CoT still helps, suggesting CoT may exploit hidden patterns.

15. **Towards Reasoning Era: Long CoT Survey** (chen2025_long_cot_survey.pdf)
    - Authors: Qiguang Chen, Libo Qin, Jinhao Liu, et al.
    - Year: 2025
    - arXiv: 2503.09567
    - Why relevant: Comprehensive survey of long CoT; Story CoT produces longer traces.

16. **Towards Better CoT Prompting Strategies: A Survey** (yu2023_cot_strategies_survey.pdf)
    - Authors: Zihan Yu, Liang He, Zhen Wu, et al.
    - Year: 2023
    - arXiv: 2310.04959
    - Why relevant: Taxonomy of CoT strategies.
